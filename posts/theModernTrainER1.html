The modern train - ER1

Versions

SBB (Switzerland)
Swiss operator SBB was the first to use the trains. SBB has 50 six-car trains and 24 four-car trains with the designation RABe 511, of which 37 six-car trains are used in the suburban trains around Zurich and the rest in regional trains throughout Switzerland. The trains have a top speed of 160 km/h and have 120 + 415 and 60 + 277 seats (first and second class, respectively).

BLS (Switzerland)
Swiss operator BLS has 31 four-car trains, designated RABe 515, which are used for Bern's suburban trains. The trains have 335 seats in both first and second class, and can accommodate up to 556 standing passengers. The trains are locally known as MUTZ, an acronym for "Moderner, Universeller TriebZug" (modern, universal motor coach).

WESTbahn (Austria)
Operator WESTbahn acquired 17 train sets between 2011 and 2017 for fast service between Vienna and Salzburg. They were sold to German railway company Deutsche Bahn in 2019.

CFL (Luxembourg)
In October 2010, Chemins de Fer Luxembourgeois (CFL) ordered eight three-car trains worth 60 million euros. The trains are 80 meters long and have 284 seats, including 29 in first class, as well as flexible spaces for up to 24 bicycles and three wheelchairs. To be able to operate in neighboring countries, the trains are equipped with ETCS and can run on both 25 kV 50 Hz and 15 kV 16.7 Hz alternating current. In December 2014, they were put into service on the Luxembourg-Koblenz route, and in March 2015, the government of Luxembourg decided to buy eleven more trains of the same type.

ODEG (Germany)
Ostdeutsche Eisenbahn (ODEG) ordered 16 trains in 2010. They were put into service in 2013 on lines in eastern Germany.

DB (Germany)
From 2019, Deutsche Bahn (DB) will take over 17 trains from Austrian train operator Westbahn. They will be renovated inside and used as intercity trains on the Rostock-Berlin-Dresden line.

Caltrain (USA)
In 2016, California commuter rail company Caltrain ordered 16 six-car trains with an option for 16 more. The trains will be used on the San Francisco-San Jose line in the US and will have a top speed of 177 km/h. The first trains are scheduled to be delivered in 2019 and are expected to be put into service in 2020. However, this has been delayed until 2022 due to the delayed electrification of the line.

Mälardalstrafik (Sweden)
For Mälartåg, regional trains in the Mälardalen region, Mälardalstrafik has 53 trains with the type designation ER1. The first order, placed in 2016, was for 33 trains and was worth about SEK 3.5billion. The first trains were put into service on December 15, 2019. The remaining 20 trains will be delivered in 2020-2021. The trains will be used on the lines between Eskilstuna and Södertälje, and between Västerås and Stockholm.

Other operators
The train has also been sold to other operators in Europe and North America, including Arriva in Denmark, RegioJet in the Czech Republic, and GoBé in Ireland. In total, over 1000 trains have been ordered and delivered to operators in 13 countries.
